The central point

Below-Market Rents Have a Measurable Cost to the Building's Value

Maintaining rents below market preserved excellent long-term tenancies. It also constrained the building's income, and a building's value is a direct function of its income. The figures below quantify that gap and outline the plan to close it on the renovated units while honoring commitments to existing tenants.

Anticipated questions

Prepared Responses

Are rents being raised on long-term tenants?

Existing tenants are not part of the repositioning. Rents move toward market only on turnover — as units vacate and are renovated — rather than through increases imposed on current long-term residents.

Does the below-market rent position affect the building's value?

Yes. Value follows income. Below-market rents were a deliberate trade-off the family chose, and the plan on this page addresses it through unit turnover rather than through increases on existing tenancies.

When will the building reach full occupancy?

Lease-up follows the construction delivery schedule: units are leased as they are completed and released. The forecast on the Financials page stages the remaining units, and this section is updated as each is delivered.

The approach

Invest in the Channels Where Renters Search; Forgo the Rest

With eight units to lease, marketing is a near-term expenditure with a measurable return: cost per lease. Below are the channels we recommend funding, the expected return from each, and one channel we recommend declining.

Recommended channels

Three Recommended Channels; One Not Recommended

Recommended

Zillow package

The platform where the largest share of Seattle renters begin their search. Paid placement keeps our eight availabilities at the top of the feed. Return framing: cost ÷ expected leases = cost per lease, measured against one month's rent.

Recommended

Google drip / digital campaign

Search and display advertising targeting "apartments Northgate / Pinehurst" intent, directed to the building's own website. This builds a lead pipeline the building owns rather than rents.

Recommended

EDDM / neighborhood mailer

Every-door direct mail across surrounding postal routes. The cost per household is low, and renters frequently relocate within the neighborhood where they already live.

Not recommended

Apartments.com

Its audience and pricing are oriented toward large, amenity-rich complexes; for a 21-unit building, the cost per qualified lead is not economically justified.

The Building's Website Is a Long-Term Asset

  • Owned, not rented. Listing sites carry an indefinite monthly cost; the building's own site and its Google presence appreciate over time.

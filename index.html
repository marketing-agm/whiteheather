The 2027 Financing Decision

At stabilization: keep paying down the construction line, or refinance. The pro forma models both side by side.

Path A — pay down the line
  • Already in place; no closing costs
  • ≈$80,000/yr interest-only carry — 8% on ≈$1.0M
  • Full payoff in ≈4.2 years at best; realistically 5–7
  • No capital returned; floating-rate risk throughout
Path B — refinance at stabilization
  • $1,500,000 permanent loan — ≈6.5%, 30-year amortization
  • ≈$113,800 annual debt service, fixed and amortizing
  • Retires the line entirely; ≈$500,000 returned at closing
  • ≈2.53× debt-service coverage at stabilization

The break-even

Debt service differs by roughly $34,000 a year. The $500,000 returned at closing, at a conservative 6%, is worth about $30,000 a year — making the refinance roughly cost-neutral on cash flow while delivering immediate liquidity. The longer the pay-down runs, the wider that gap becomes.

Anticipated Questions

Are rents being raised on long-term tenants?

Existing tenants are not part of the repositioning. Rents move toward market only on turnover — as units vacate and are renovated — rather than through increases imposed on current long-term residents.

Does the below-market rent position affect the building's value?

Yes. Value follows income. Below-market rents were a deliberate trade-off the family chose, and the plan on this page addresses it through unit turnover rather than through increases on existing tenancies.

When will the building reach full occupancy?

Lease-up follows the construction delivery schedule: units are leased as they are completed and released. The forecast on the Financials page stages the remaining units, and this section is updated as each is delivered.

The takeaway

The Website as a Long-Term Asset

  • Owned, not rented. Stop paying a listing platform and its traffic stops. SEO converts marketing expense into a long-term digital asset that keeps producing.
  • Search visibility. Searches for "White Heather Apartments" and neighborhood phrases like "apartments Northgate / Pinehurst" should land on our page, our photographs, and our application link — not a listing aggregator's.
  • Better-qualified leads. Website prospects sought the property out intentionally; they arrive more educated and convert to tours and leases at higher rates.
  • Measured like an investment. Lead-source mix, lead-to-tour and tour-to-lease conversion, cost per lease by channel, days vacant, and renewal rate — reported in the owner portal as channels are booked.

Resident Transfer Program

Turnover is among the costliest events in a small building. Renovated units are therefore offered to existing residents first — at a preferred in-house rate, before public advertising — keeping good tenants, upgrading the resident profile, and filling vacancies faster.

  • How it works. Vacant renovated units are offered at a below-market, in-house rate. The existing security deposit is returned and applied toward the new unit's deposit, case-by-case — the renovated unit's condition keeps exposure low.
  • Completed. Unit 304's resident transferred into renovated 307 at $2,350; 304 then entered Phase 2 renovation.
  • In motion. Unit 106's resident has asked to move into 107 once renovated, at the market rate; unit 207 has been offered a transfer and is considering it.
  • Honored in place. Long-term, water-event-affected residents — including 101, 103, and 303 — have chosen to stay. Where a market increase is not feasible, their tenancy is respected and repositioning waits for natural turnover.

Why Zillow Leads the Mix

  • Lead volume. The massive consumer audience across Zillow, Trulia, and HotPads consistently generates the most inquiries.
  • Occupancy speed. The strongest tool when the objective is filling vacancies and lease-ups quickly.
  • Market intelligence. Shows which units draw attention, whether pricing and concessions are working, and whether renovations are producing rent premiums.
  • Small-building coverage. Captures walk-ups, fourplexes, and independent landlords — White Heather's actual neighborhood competitors appear more completely on Zillow.
  • Concession visibility. Pricing and move-in specials are prominent, so effective-rent marketing performs well.

Website, SEO & Google Campaign — The Owned Pipeline

Relying only on listing platforms means they own the customer relationship — you are effectively renting attention. The building's own website, backed by SEO and a Google campaign, builds visibility the property keeps.

Cost to Launch

  • Google drip campaign. Pay-per-click at roughly $10/day — approximately $200–$250 per month. The package scales up if the directive is high leasing velocity in a short window.
  • SEO integration. Typically shows results and increased traffic within about three weeks of launch.

Why It Compounds

  • Paid traffic stops when payment stops. Zillow, Apartments.com, and Google Ads rent visibility; organic search keeps producing after the initial investment.
  • Marketing assets become searchable. Photography, tours, and neighborhood content only have lasting value when they live somewhere Google can find — searches like "apartments Northgate Seattle" become leads that cost nothing per click.
  • Website leads convert better. They sought the property out intentionally and arrive more educated, so lead-to-tour and tour-to-lease rates run higher.
